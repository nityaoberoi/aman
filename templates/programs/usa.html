The EB-5 Immigrant Investor program was created by the United States’ Congress in 1990 and is administered by the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services. It offers a path to permanent residency (Green Card) for foreigners who invest in a job-generating commercial enterprise located in a “Target Employment Area” in the US (generally a rural area, or an area with high unemployment rate). Commercial enterprises formally approved by USCIS to participate in the program are designated as “EB-5 Regional Centers”.

Foreign National Investors who wish to participate in the EB5 program must fulfill certain conditions and invest $500,000 or more in a Regional Center of their choice. These funds must be locked in for a certain period of time, generally 5 years, before the Investor, can retrieve them.

As the funds are invested, the Investor must present a petition to USCIS. The approval of this petition will grant him/her and his/her immediate family members conditional permanent residency (green card) in the US valid for two years. After two years, he/she can petition for full residency status, and after five years for US citizenship. For more information about the EB-5 immigration process, visit our FAQs page.
